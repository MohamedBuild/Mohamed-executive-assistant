## 1. Business Context

*Goal: understand the shape of the business before diagnosing anything.*

- What do they actually sell — product, service, subscription, project-based?
- Who are their customers — B2B, B2C, industry, size?
- Team structure — who does what, where are the handoffs?
- Revenue stage — are they growing, stable, stretched?
- What's the growth plan — more clients, bigger clients, new markets?

- Where does your data live — spreadsheets, docs, tools, or all three?

## 5. Data Hygiene

*Goal: assess whether their data is good enough to automate against. Bad data = broken automations.*

- Where does your client/contact data live? Is it one place or several?
- If I asked you "how many active clients do you have" — where would you look?
- How clean is that data — duplicates, missing fields, outdated records?
- Do you have standard formats for things like dates, names, statuses?
- Has bad data ever caused a problem — wrong thing sent, something missed?

## 6. Team & Tech Literacy

*Goal: assess who will actually run these automations after handoff. Automation without an owner fails.*

- Who on the team is most comfortable with tools and technology?
- Have they used automation tools before — Zapier, Make, n8n, anything?
- If something breaks after I've handed it over, who deals with it?
- Is there someone who actively enjoys this kind of work, or is it all you?
- What's the realistic tech ceiling — "happy to click buttons" or "can read a workflow diagram"?

## 7. Previous Attempts

*Goal: learn from what they've already tried. Failures reveal constraints; successes reveal what they value.*

- Have you tried automating anything before — what was it?
- What happened — did it work, did it break, did it get abandoned?
- If it failed: what do you think went wrong?
- If it worked: what made it stick?
- Is there anything they tried that I should explicitly avoid repeating?

## 8. Budget & Timeline

*Goal: calibrate recommendations to what's actually viable. Don't design a Rolls-Royce for a Corolla budget.*

- Do they have a number in mind, or are they waiting to hear a recommendation first?
- Is this a one-off project or ongoing relationship?
- Is there a deadline driving this — a hire they're trying to avoid, a launch, a growth target?
- Who makes the final call — is that person on this call?
- Have they had proposals from others? What were those like?

## 9. Goals & Success Metrics

*Goal: define what "this worked" means. Vague goals = scope creep and disappointed clients.*

- 6 months from now, how would you know this was worth it?
- Is the goal time savings, headcount reduction, fewer errors, client experience, revenue growth?
- Is there a number attached — hours saved per week, clients handled per person?
- What would make you recommend this to someone else?
- What would make you regret it?
